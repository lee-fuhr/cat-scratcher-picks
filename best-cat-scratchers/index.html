Quick answer

For most cats and homes, the SmartCat Ultimate Scratching Post is the top pick, a tall, stable sisal post that satisfies the instinct to stretch and scratch.

The PetFusion Ultimate Cat Scratcher Lounge is the best budget choice, the K&H Scratch Pad excels for cats who prefer horizontal scratching, and Frisco’s cat trees offer extra climbing and condo space for active felines.

Cats scratch to mark territory, stretch muscles, and keep their claws healthy. A good scratcher saves your sofa and gives your cat an outlet for that deep-seated instinct. The best scratchers combine a material cats love (sisal, corrugated cardboard, or carpet), a stable base, and a size that lets them fully extend. When choosing, think about your cat’s preferred scratching angle: some like to stretch upward on a vertical post, others prefer a horizontal pad, and many enjoy a mix. Durability matters, sisal and thick cardboard last longer. Also consider where the scratcher goes: a tall post needs floor space near a favorite hangout, while a flat pad can tuck into a corner.
